# ALT.ZINE

Look, another app. Great. Just what the world needed. 

ALT.ZINE is a physical-key eReader and note editor for people who still have phones with actual, clicky buttons. If you’re using a slab of glass that costs more than a used car, this isn't for you. Go back to your gestures and haptic feedback. This is for the weirdos with D-pads and numpads who want to actually feel something when they press a button.

## What is it?
It reads stuff. You can write stuff. It’s a Zine, but "Alt." Get it? It doesn’t have ads, it doesn’t track your eye movements, and it doesn't care about your "user experience." It just sits there in its dark, Matrix-green glory and waits for you to click something.

## Who is it for?
*   People with physical keyboards/numpads on Android.
*   People who hate touching their screen because fingerprints are gross.
*   People who think a 3x3 grid is the peak of UI design.
*   Fl0k1.

## Why does it exist?
Because modern Android apps assume you have thumbs the size of toothpicks and the precision of a surgeon. We have a numpad. We have a D-pad. Why the hell aren't we using them? So, we built this. Now we're using them. Happy?

## Minimal Features (Because we’re lazy)
*   **A 3x3 Grid:** It’s like a numpad, but on your screen. Revolutionary.
*   **Notes:** You can type words. They save when you hit 'Back'. Don't forget, or they’re gone. Life is hard.
*   **Last Note:** One button to jump back into your last half-finished thought.
*   **List View:** Navigate your notes with a D-pad like it’s 2004. Delete them with a left click if they're embarrassing.
*   **eReader:** (Coming soon, maybe. Depends on how much coffee we have.)

## How to use it (If you can't figure it out)
1.  **Press a number:** Go to a page.
2.  **Press Back:** Go Home.
3.  **In the List:** ↑↓ to move, → to edit, ← to delete.
4.  **Don't complain:** It works on my machine.

If it crashes, it’s probably your phone's fault. Or yours. Mostly yours.

Stay alt. Or don't. I don't give a shit.
